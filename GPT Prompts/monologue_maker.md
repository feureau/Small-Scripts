\# PROMPT FOR COMMENTARY PIECE

I'm going to give you a monologue on a topic. It's in the style of a video commentary—opinionated, conversational, critical of corporate practices. I want you to help me turn this into a written piece with the following specifications:

\#\# YOUR TASK

Take my monologue and use it as source material to write a complete, structured commentary piece. The piece should capture the spirit and tone of what I wrote while being entirely original in phrasing, examples, and execution.

\#\# THE TOPIC

The topic will be whatever I wrote about in the monologue. Use that as your starting point.

\#\# WHAT I NEED FROM YOU

1\. \*\*Research the topic.\*\* Find specific facts, numbers, dates, prices, company names, and historical context related to what I'm talking about. Pull from news articles, official announcements, public records, and credible sources.

2\. \*\*Structure the piece\*\* in this order:  
   \- Opening hook (big picture statement about the trend)  
   \- Introduce the specific example  
   \- Break down the deal with actual numbers  
   \- Expose hidden costs and fine print  
   \- Analyze who this targets and why it's predatory  
   \- Acknowledge any counter-arguments (then explain why they don't hold up)  
   \- Connect to broader trend (other examples in other industries)  
   \- Offer some action or perspective on what to do about it  
   \- Quick closing

3\. \*\*Use my voice and style\*\* as shown in the monologue—conversational, direct,偶尔 sarcastic,偶尔 angry, but always clear. Write like a person talking, not an essay.

4\. \*\*Avoid plagiarism completely:\*\*  
   \- Do not copy any phrases or sentences from my monologue  
   \- Do not copy from the penguinz0 transcript or any other source  
   \- Create original metaphors, analogies, and turns of phrase  
   \- If using a well-known concept (like "you will own nothing"), paraphrase it or use it sparingly  
   \- Write everything in your own words based on the research

5\. \*\*Include specific numbers and details\*\* from your research—prices, totals, comparisons, dates, company background. The more concrete, the better.

6\. \*\*End with something useful\*\*—not just complaining. A resource, a perspective, a call to action.

\#\# OUTPUT FORMAT

Give me a complete, ready-to-use written piece. No placeholders, no brackets to fill in. Just the finished text.
