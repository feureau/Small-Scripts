#### **THE FUN TO LISTEN TO REQUIREMENTS**


A script that is only argument is compelling the way a documentary is compelling. It is not fun to be with. The following techniques must be distributed throughout the script — not concentrated in the emotional resolution section.

**Technique 1 — Genuine reactions before analysis:** When something genuinely surprising or absurd lands, let the speaker experience it slightly before the analysis arrives. The reaction happens before the explanation. Not performed surprise — the natural order of encountering something unexpected.

**Technique 2 — Absurdity acknowledged before moving past it:** Absurdity needs a beat. Not a punchline — just the space for the listener to recognize it and enjoy it before the analysis continues. Absurd details that are moved past at the same pace as everything else lose their power.

**Technique 3 — The speaker's personality in the tangents:** Moments where the speaker's personal voice breaks through — not arguing anything, just being themselves for a moment. Genuine enthusiasm, genuine disgust, genuine affection for a specific detail. These moments make the serious parts land harder by contrast. They must be distributed throughout the script, not saved for the end.

**Technique 4 — Varying sentence energy:** Fast and loose sections, slow and deliberate sections, genuinely funny moments, genuinely heavy moments. This variation must be built into the text itself through paragraph length and clause density — not only indicated in delivery notes.

**Technique 5 — Giving the listener something to do:** Occasional moments that make the listener feel like they're in the room — a brief aside, a shared observation, a moment where the speaker clearly finds something delightful or infuriating. The listener should feel occasionally like a participant not a recipient.

**Technique 6 — Humor that serves the argument:** Observations that are both funny and true, where laughing at the observation means accepting the point it makes. The humor is the argument, not decoration around it. Identify the moments in the source material where this is possible and use them.

**The personality-to-argument ratio:** The ratio established in Step 3G must be maintained throughout the script. If the reference transcript has moments of personality distributed every two to three minutes, the script must have the same. Mark where these moments fall in the script before writing — don't leave them to emerge naturally, because they won't.
