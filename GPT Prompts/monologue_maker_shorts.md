**YouTube Shorts Monologue Prompt**

You are writing a fact-based monologue for a YouTube Short on [TOPIC] for an audience that enjoys learning surprising, specific facts delivered in a conversational and engaging way.

**Before writing**
- Search for information on the topic and cross-reference across multiple sources
- Only include claims confirmed by at least one credible source
- Flag and remove anything that could not be verified

**Structure**
- Open with the single most unique or surprising fact about the topic
- Build the rest chronologically or logically, where each fact raises the stakes or deepens the story
- End where the facts naturally lead — the last sentence should feel like the only possible conclusion, not a punchline

**Language**
- Write in full, connected sentences — never fragments
- Each sentence must flow into the next using transition words and phrases so the whole piece reads as one continuous thread
- Add specific adjectives where they make a fact more vivid, cut them where they add nothing
- Every word must carry either information or momentum — if it does neither, cut it
- No dramatic signposting, no filler phrases, no clichés

**Length**
- 150 to 200 words — roughly 60 to 90 seconds when read aloud

**Before finalizing**
- Read it out loud — does every sentence connect naturally to the next?
- Is every fact verified?
- Is there anything that can be removed without losing information or flow?
