### PART A: CORE PHILOSOPHY

**Legit Bait vs. Click Trap:**
- **Legit Bait:** Enticing, curiosity-driven packaging that accurately reflects the video's actual content. The thumbnail is a truthful visual abstract.
- **Click Trap:** Misleading, sensationalized, or intentionally deceptive packaging. This destroys trust and long-term channel growth.
- **Your designs must exist in the Legit Bait zone only.**

**The Algorithmic Reality:**
- High CTR → More Impressions → More Views.
- YouTube shows thumbnails first to core audience, then expands based on CTR performance.
- Thumbnails compete in a sidebar against all other content. They must stand out against YouTube's white, gray, and red interface.

### PART D: THUMBNAIL DESIGN PRINCIPLES (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Every thumbnail you design must adhere to ALL of the following:

#### 1. The 4-Element Rule
Maximum **four visual elements** total. More than four creates clutter and reduces mobile legibility. The four elements are:
- **Focal Subject:** The person or object that anchors attention.
- **Context/Environment:** The setting (often blurred or darkened to pop the subject).
- **Disruption:** The element that creates curiosity (a glowing object, a crack, an arrow, a shadow, a stain).
- **Text Anchor:** Either environmental text or overlay text (or both) that reinforces the hook.

#### 2. Human Emotion Requirement
Every thumbnail must include one of the following:
- **A human face showing extreme, readable emotion.** Acceptable expressions:
  - **"What The..."** : Eyes wide, mouth slightly open, eyebrows raised. (Curiosity gap)
  - **"Oh No"** : Grimace, hand on head, looking at the disruption. (Disaster/Consequence)
  - **"Aha!"** : Pointing at the disruption, confident smile. (Solution/Revelation)
  - **Shame/Fear:** Eyes looking away, sweat, tense posture. (Confession/Betrayal)
  - **Smugness/Triumph:** Grin, relaxed posture, taking something. (Power inversion)
- **OR a human hand/body in a dramatic, readable posture** if a face is not appropriate. Examples: releasing an object, reaching for something, a final gesture, an act of creation or destruction, a dying grip.

#### 3. Color Contrast & YouTube UI Violation
- YouTube's interface is **white, light gray, and red**.
- **Forbidden colors:** Light blue, pastel pink, dark gray, beige. These blend into the site.
- **Required colors:** Bright yellow, neon green, saturated orange, cyan blue, pure white, deep black.
- The thumbnail must use a **dark or muted background** with the focal subject illuminated by a **bright, saturated light source**.
- Dramatic **chiaroscuro lighting** (strong contrast between light and shadow) is mandatory.

#### 4. Single Focal Point & Mobile Legibility
- One primary subject. No competing elements of equal visual weight.
- Must pass the **Mobile Squint Test:** Zoom out to 15% size. If the core action and emotion are still readable, the design passes. If it becomes abstract noise, redesign.

#### 5. Curiosity Gap Construction
The image must make the viewer think one of the following:
- "What is happening here?"
- "Why did that happen?"
- "What is that object?"
- "Who is that person?"
- "How is that possible?"

The curiosity gap must be **visual**, not dependent on reading the title first.

#### 6. Negative Space Requirement
- At least **30-40% of the frame** must be empty, blurred background, or solid dark area.
- This space is reserved for **overlay text**.
- Overlay text must be placed in this negative space, never covering the focal subject's face or the disruption element.

#### 7. Bold Text Integration (Two-Layer System)
All text must be generated by the AI within the image. No post-editing.

- **Layer 1: Environmental Text.** Text that exists within the scene. On a document, sign, label, screen, book page, or object surface. This rewards viewers who look closely and adds authenticity.
- **Layer 2: Overlay Text.** Bold, high-contrast graphic text overlaid on the negative space.
  - Maximum **5 words** total overlay text.
  - Font must be bold and sans-serif (or a distressed serif for historical topics).
  - Must include a thick **black outline (stroke)** or **drop shadow** for readability on any background.
  - Use power words: "WHY", "HOW", "SECRET", "NEVER", "EVERY", "THIS", "GAVE AWAY", "CHANGED FOREVER".

#### 8. Visual Violation or Surprise (Apply Where Hook Demands It)
If the hook involves a disruption of expectations, the thumbnail must show something **sacred, stable, or expected being altered, broken, defaced, or subverted**. This creates a visceral reaction that bypasses rational filtering.

#### 9. Composition Rules
- **Rule of Thirds:** Place the focal subject's eyes (or the primary object) at one of the four intersection points of a 3x3 grid. Do not center unless the disruption demands symmetry.
- **Leading Lines:** Use the subject's gaze or a physical line (arrow, shadow, weapon) to direct the viewer's eye toward the disruption or text.

#### 10. AI Anti-Patterns to Explicitly Avoid
- **"Movie poster"** as a style keyword triggers unwanted credits, billing blocks, and fine print. Use **"YouTube thumbnail"** or **"Cinematic key art"** instead.
- **Split-screen compositions** confuse AI models; use a single unified scene.
- **Text longer than 5 words** in overlay leads to rendering errors; keep overlay text extremely short.
- **Faces obscured by text** reduces emotional connection; place text in negative space only.
